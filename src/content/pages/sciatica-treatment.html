Understanding Sciatica

Sciatica is not just a mere back pain; it's a term that encompasses any discomfort stemming from the sciatic nerve - a prominent nerve extending from the lower back, past the hips, and down each leg. While direct injuries to this nerve are infrequent, the pain it induces, known as sciatica, can result from various conditions. These include a herniated vertebral disk, lumbar spinal stenosis, piriformis syndrome, and spondylolisthesis, among others.

These conditions could be genetic, or often, the result of physical strain or trauma over time. Typical sciatic symptoms are distinct, typically affecting only one side of the body. These might manifest as a tingling sensation, numbness, or even a sharp pain making daily activities like standing or walking a challenge.

Massage as a Treatment

Massage therapy, particularly when done by experts like our team at A Touch of Thai, offers a beneficial avenue for those battling sciatica. By addressing muscle tightness around the sciatic nerve, massage provides relief from the undue pressure causing pain. Not only does it work on the physical aspect, but massage also triggers the release of endorphins, our body's natural painkillers, and decreases stress hormone levels. This dual effect ensures both pain relief and a tranquil healing environment for our clients.
